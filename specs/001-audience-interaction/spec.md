## User Scenarios & Testing _(mandatory)_

### User Story 1 - Audience Joins via QR Code and Sees Contextual Interactions (Priority: P1)

An audience member at the conference sees a QR code displayed on the
presenter's slide. They scan it with their phone and are taken to a
mobile-friendly participation page. No sign-up or login is required.
The page shows interactive content relevant to whichever slide the
presenter is currently on: when the presenter advances to a poll
slide, the poll automatically appears on audience devices; when
the presenter moves to a non-interactive slide, the page shows the
persistent question feed. The audience never needs to manually
navigate — the experience is driven by the presenter's slide
changes.

**Why this priority**: This is the foundation for all other
interactive features. Without a way for the audience to connect and
have their view driven by the presenter's current slide, polls and
Q&A have no delivery channel.

**Independent Test**: Can be fully tested by scanning the QR code on
a phone, verifying the participation page loads, and confirming that
when the presenter moves to a poll slide the poll appears on the
phone, and when the presenter moves away the phone returns to the
question feed.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** the presenter is on any slide, **When** an audience
   member scans the QR code, **Then** a mobile-friendly
   participation page loads without requiring login or sign-up.
2. **Given** an audience member has the participation page open,
   **When** the presenter navigates to a slide with a poll, **Then**
   the poll question and options appear on the audience member's
   device within 2 seconds.
3. **Given** an audience member has the participation page open and
   a poll is showing, **When** the presenter advances past the poll
   slide, **Then** the audience member's device transitions back to
   the persistent question feed.
4. **Given** an audience member has the participation page open,
   **When** the backend is temporarily unreachable, **Then** the
   page displays a "Connection lost — reconnecting..." message
   and automatically retries without the user needing to refresh.

### User Story 2 - Presenter Runs a Live Poll (Priority: P2)

The presenter triggers a poll on a specific slide during the talk.
Audience members see the poll question and answer options appear on
their devices and can tap to vote. The presenter's slide shows a
live-updating bar chart of results. Once the presenter closes the
poll, final results are locked and remain visible.

The initial poll for this presentation asks: "What's your AI
planning approach?" with three options: "I spec my kits",
"Plan mode, more like pain mode", and "WTF AI can plan?".

**Why this priority**: Polls are the most visible and engaging
interactive feature. They create an early "wow" moment and get the
audience actively participating from the start.

**Independent Test**: Can be fully tested by opening the poll on
the presenter slide, voting from a phone, and verifying the results
update live on both the presenter slide and all audience devices.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** the presenter navigates to a poll slide, **When** the
   poll is active, **Then** audience members see the question and
   options on their devices and can tap to cast a vote.
2. **Given** an audience member has voted, **When** other votes
   arrive, **Then** the result chart on the presenter's slide
   updates in real time (within 2 seconds).
3. **Given** an audience member has already voted on a poll,
   **When** they try to vote again on the same poll, **Then** the
   system replaces their previous vote (change-your-mind allowed,
   but only one active vote per person per poll).
4. **Given** the presenter closes a poll, **When** an audience
   member tries to vote, **Then** the system rejects the vote and
   the audience member sees a "Poll closed" message.
5. **Given** the backend is unreachable, **When** the presenter
   navigates to a poll slide, **Then** the slide still renders
   correctly with a "Poll unavailable — check back shortly"
   message instead of live results.

### User Story 3 - Audience Submits and Upvotes Questions (Priority: P3)

At any point during the presentation, an audience member can type a
question on their device and submit it. All submitted questions
appear in a shared question feed visible to every audience member.
Anyone can upvote or downvote questions to surface the most popular
ones. The presenter sees the top questions sorted by net votes on
the final "Thank You" slide (or a dedicated Q&A slide) and
addresses them in order.

**Why this priority**: Q&A is valuable but the presentation can
succeed without it. It layers on top of the slide-sync and poll
infrastructure.

**Independent Test**: Can be fully tested by submitting a question
from one device, upvoting it from another device, and verifying the
question list updates on all connected devices in real time.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** an audience member is on the participation page,
   **When** they type a question and tap submit, **Then** the
   question appears in the shared question feed on all connected
   devices within 2 seconds.
2. **Given** the question feed contains multiple questions,
   **When** an audience member taps the upvote button on a
   question, **Then** the vote count increments and the feed
   re-sorts by net votes in real time.
3. **Given** an audience member has already upvoted a question,
   **When** they tap upvote again, **Then** their vote is removed
   (toggle behavior).
4. **Given** the question feed is active, **When** the presenter
   navigates to the Q&A slide, **Then** the presenter's slide
   displays the top questions sorted by net votes.
5. **Given** a question has been submitted, **When** the presenter
   marks it as "answered", **Then** the question is visually
   distinguished from unanswered questions on all devices.

### User Story 4 - Anti-Abuse Protections (Priority: P2)

The audience consists of developers who are likely to test
boundaries. The system prevents common abuse vectors: spam flooding,
offensive content injection, vote manipulation, and attempts to
interfere with other users' experience. All this happens without
requiring users to log in, while still uniquely identifying devices
to enforce per-device limits.

**Why this priority**: Same priority as polls because abuse
protections must be in place before any interactive feature goes
live with a technical audience. Shipping polls or Q&A without these
safeguards would risk disrupting the presentation itself.

**Independent Test**: Can be tested by attempting rapid-fire
question submissions, duplicate voting from the same device, and
verifying rate limits and deduplication enforce correctly.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a device has submitted 3 questions within 1 minute,
   **When** it attempts to submit a 4th, **Then** the system
   rejects the submission and shows a "Slow down — try again in a
   moment" message.
2. **Given** a device has cast a vote on a poll, **When** the same
   device attempts to cast an additional distinct vote on the same
   poll, **Then** the system replaces the previous vote (one vote
   per device per poll).
3. **Given** a question is submitted, **When** it exceeds 300
   characters, **Then** the system rejects it with a "Question too
   long" message.
4. **Given** a question is submitted, **When** it contains content
   that consists solely of repeated characters, excessive
   whitespace, or is otherwise clearly not a genuine question,
   **Then** the system rejects it.
5. **Given** a device attempts to upvote or downvote more than 30
   questions within 1 minute, **When** the limit is exceeded,
   **Then** further votes from that device are silently throttled
   until the window resets.

### Edge Cases

- What happens when 200+ audience members connect simultaneously?
  The system MUST handle at least 200 concurrent connections without
  degradation (typical conference room size).
- What happens when an audience member's phone loses connectivity
  mid-poll? Their participation page reconnects automatically and
  restores the current state (poll still open, their prior vote
  preserved).
- What happens when the presenter refreshes their browser during a
  poll? Poll state persists server-side; the presenter's slide
  resumes showing current results.
- What happens when an audience member opens multiple tabs/devices?
  Each device is treated independently for rate limiting but shares
  no cross-device session.
- What happens when the presenter has not connected yet? The
  participation page shows a "Presentation hasn't started yet —
  hang tight" holding state until the presenter loads the deck and
  the session auto-activates.
- What happens when the presenter resets mid-session? All poll
  votes, questions, and question votes are cleared. Connected
  audience devices see their state reset (empty question feed,
  polls cleared). Poll definitions remain intact.
- What happens if someone submits a question with HTML or script
  tags? All user-submitted content MUST be rendered as plain text,
  never interpreted as markup.
- What happens if an audience member discovers the presenter token?
  The token can be rotated by the presenter at any time. Service
  endpoints enforce the token server-side, so client-side discovery
  alone does not grant persistent access if the token is changed.

## Requirements _(mandatory)_

### Functional Requirements

- **FR-001**: System MUST display a QR code on a designated
  presenter slide that links to the audience participation page.
- **FR-002**: The audience participation page MUST load and be
  usable without any sign-up, login, or authentication.
- **FR-003**: System MUST broadcast the current slide context to all
  connected audience devices in real time (within 2 seconds), so
  that the participation page automatically shows the relevant
  interaction (poll on poll slides, question feed otherwise).
- **FR-004**: System MUST support polls with multiple-choice options
  displayed on audience devices. Polls are pre-defined in the slide
  markdown and seeded to the backend when the session starts. A poll
  becomes active when the presenter navigates to its slide.
- **FR-005**: Poll results MUST update live on the presenter's
  slide as votes arrive.
- **FR-006**: System MUST enforce one active vote per device per
  poll, allowing vote changes until the poll is closed.
- **FR-007**: System MUST allow audience members to submit free-text
  questions at any time during the presentation.
- **FR-008**: System MUST allow audience members to upvote or
  downvote any submitted question (toggle: tap again to undo).
- **FR-009**: Questions MUST be sorted by net votes (upvotes minus
  downvotes) in the feed, with real-time re-sorting.
- **FR-010**: System MUST provide a presenter view of top questions
  sorted by net votes for the Q&A slide.
- **FR-011**: The presenter MUST be able to mark questions as
  "answered".
- **FR-012**: All server-side operations MUST be handled through
  secured service endpoints — the browser MUST NOT execute database
  queries directly.
- **FR-013**: System MUST enforce rate limits: maximum 3 question
  submissions per device per minute, maximum 30 votes per device
  per minute.
- **FR-014**: User-submitted text MUST be limited to 300 characters
  and MUST be rendered as plain text (no HTML/script execution).
- **FR-015**: System MUST reject submissions that consist of
  repeated characters, excessive whitespace, or zero meaningful
  content.
- **FR-016**: The presentation slides MUST remain fully functional
  (navigation, content, notes) when the interactive backend is
  unavailable — interactive elements degrade gracefully with clear
  messaging.
- **FR-017**: System MUST assign a persistent anonymous device
  identifier to each connecting device (browser fingerprint or
  generated token) to enforce per-device limits without requiring
  login.
- **FR-018**: The audience participation page MUST be mobile-first
  and usable on screens 320px and wider.
- **FR-019**: Presenter actions (close poll, mark question answered,
  broadcast slide changes) MUST require a pre-configured secret
  token. The token is passed via the presenter URL and persisted in
  the browser's local storage so it only needs to be entered once.
- **FR-020**: Service endpoints MUST reject presenter-level
  operations when the request does not include a valid presenter
  token.
- **FR-021**: A session MUST be automatically created and activated
  when the presenter first loads the deck with a valid token. No
  manual "start" step is required.
- **FR-022**: The presenter MUST be able to manually end a session.
  Once ended, the audience participation page shows a
  "Presentation has ended — thanks for joining!" message and no
  further submissions or votes are accepted.
- **FR-023**: The presenter MUST be able to reset a session, which
  clears all poll votes, questions, and question votes while
  preserving poll definitions and session configuration. This
  enables full dry-run testing before the live talk.
- **FR-024**: The presenter MUST be able to hide individual
  questions. Hidden questions MUST be removed from the audience
  feed immediately but remain visible (marked as hidden) in the
  presenter view for reference.

### Key Entities

- **Session**: Represents a single presentation event. Has a status
  (active / ended), current slide number, and creation timestamp.
  Auto-created on first presenter connect. Supports a reset
  operation that clears all child data (votes, questions) while
  preserving poll definitions.
- **Poll**: Belongs to a session and is tied to a specific slide.
  Has a question, ordered list of options, and status (open /
  closed). One poll per slide maximum. Pre-defined in slide
  markdown and seeded to the backend on session start. Becomes
  active when the presenter navigates to its slide.
- **PollVote**: Records a single device's vote on a poll. Linked to
  a poll and a device identifier. One vote per device per poll.
- **Question**: Submitted by an audience device within a session.
  Has text content, a device identifier, submission timestamp,
  net vote score, answered status, and hidden status. Hidden
  questions are excluded from the audience feed but visible to the
  presenter.
- **QuestionVote**: Records a single device's vote (up or down) on
  a question. One vote per device per question.

## Success Criteria _(mandatory)_

### Measurable Outcomes

- **SC-001**: 90% of audience members can scan the QR code and
  reach the participation page within 15 seconds on a mobile
  device.
- **SC-002**: Slide-sync updates appear on connected audience
  devices within 2 seconds of the presenter changing slides.
- **SC-003**: Poll results update live on the presenter's slide
  within 2 seconds of a vote being cast.
- **SC-004**: System handles 200 concurrent connected devices
  without visible degradation in response times.
- **SC-005**: No audience member can submit more than 3 questions
  per minute or cast more than 30 votes per minute.
- **SC-006**: The presentation slides function identically (content,
  navigation, notes) when the interactive backend is offline.
- **SC-007**: All user-submitted content renders as plain text;
  no injected markup or scripts execute.
- **SC-008**: 80% of connected audience members interact with at
  least one feature (poll or question) during the presentation.

## Clarifications

### Session 2026-02-27

- Q: Should AI-generated talking points for Q&A be part of this spec? → A: Deferred to a separate feature spec. Strategy note: begin generating talking points for top 3–5 questions when the presenter reaches the second- or third-to-last slide, so they are ready by the Q&A slide. This avoids real-time latency pressure.
- Q: How are presenter actions authorized vs. audience actions? → A: Shared secret token in the presenter URL (e.g., `/presenter?token=abc123`). Token is persisted in the browser's local storage after first entry, so the presenter does not need to re-enter it on subsequent visits.
- Q: How is a session created, started, and ended? → A: Auto-start on presenter connect — session is created and activated when the presenter first loads the deck with a valid token; presenter manually ends it. The presenter also has a manual "reset" action that wipes all ephemeral data (poll votes, questions, question votes) to allow full dry-run testing before the live talk.
- Q: Can the presenter moderate inappropriate questions that pass automated filters? → A: Yes — presenter can hide individual questions. Hidden questions are removed from the audience feed but remain visible (marked as hidden) in the presenter view.
- Q: Are polls pre-defined or created dynamically at runtime? → A: Pre-defined in the slide markdown. Poll definitions are declared in the slide content and seeded to the backend when the session starts. No runtime poll-creation UI is needed.

## Assumptions

- The conference venue provides WiFi adequate for 200+ simultaneous
  mobile connections.
- The presenter runs the presentation from a laptop with reliable
  internet connectivity.
- Audience devices are modern smartphones with current browser
  versions (iOS Safari 16+, Chrome for Android 110+).
- Session data (polls, questions, votes) does not need to persist
  beyond the day of the presentation — it is ephemeral event data.
- A single presentation session runs at a time; multi-session
  support is not required.
- The QR code URL is stable and can be pre-printed on physical
  materials if desired.
